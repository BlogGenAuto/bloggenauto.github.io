How to Wall Mount a Bike for Different Types: E-bikes to Roadies

Not all bikes are created equal. In 2026, the rise of heavy e-bikes has changed the conversation on how to wall mount a bike. Lifting a 25kg commuter bike onto a high hook is not feasible for everyone. Modern space saving bike storage now incorporates 'no-lift' designs.

The No-Lift Technique for E-Bikes

By using the rear brake to pop the bike onto its back wheel, you can simply roll it forward and hook the front wheel without any heavy lifting. This makes an e-bike wall mount accessible for older riders or those with limited strength. Avoiding common bike wall hook mistakes like incorrect mounting height is crucial for this technique to work.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best way to hang a bike on a wall?

The best way to hang a bike on a wall in 2026 is vertically using a rail-mounted system. This method saves the most floor space by allowing bikes to hang perpendicularly to the wall. Using a rail system like GearHooks also allows for easy adjustments to height and spacing as your bike collection evolves.

Do it yourself bike wall mount?

A DIY bike wall mount is possible, but for multiple bikes, a professional rail system is much safer. DIY wood mounts can degrade over time or fail under the weight of modern e-bikes. Professional systems come with tested, heavy-duty fixings that ensure your bikes remain secure without damaging your walls.

Can kids with dyspraxia ride a bike?

Yes, children with dyspraxia can certainly ride bikes, often benefiting from specialized training and equipment. For families with dyspraxic riders, a 'no-lift' vertical bike rack is particularly helpful. It simplifies the process of storing the bike, removing the need for complex lifting or balancing maneuvers that might be challenging.

How to put a bike on a wall?

To put a bike on a wall, first install a horizontal rail at a height that allows the rear wheel to touch the floor or hang slightly above it. Simply lift the front wheel (or roll it up using the 'no-lift' method) and place the rim onto a padded hook. Ensure the hook is deep enough to hold the tyre securely and is wide enough for your specific tyre width.
