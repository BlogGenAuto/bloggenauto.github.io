Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good way to hang bicycles on a wall?

By far the most affordable and efficient option in 2026 is a screw-in storage hook for your wall or ceiling. This is a simple yet effective way to hang your bike off the ground and out of the way. You can use just one heavy-duty hook to hang your bike vertically by the front wheel, or two hooks to hang it horizontally for display purposes. Systems like the GearHooks B2 offer a more robust, professional alternative to basic hardware store hooks.

Can kids with dyspraxia ride a bike?

Yes, children with dyspraxia can absolutely learn to ride. It may help to take the pedals off at first and let the child push the bike with their feet as a balance bike, while their balance and confidence improves. Encourage the child to lift their feet and move a short distance whilst holding their balance. It is often easier for children with coordination challenges to balance on bikes with wider tyres, and keeping the bike organized and accessible at home helps encourage frequent, short practice sessions.

Should you hang a bike by the front or back tire?

Both the front or rear wheel are perfectly fine for vertical storage, though most cyclists prefer using the front wheel for convenience and ease of "rolling" the bike up. Just make sure your hook or hanger is properly padded with rubber or plastic so it doesn't scratch your rim. In 2026, most high-quality hooks are designed to be wheel-friendly for both carbon and alloy rims.

Can you hang a bike on a plasterboard wall?

Yes, wall mounts can be used on plasterboard (or drywall) with the help of specialized heavy-duty anchors. However, for maximum safety—especially with heavy e-bikes—it is much better to find the wooden stud or frame behind the wall and screw directly into it. If you must mount on plasterboard, ensure the fixing is rated for the weight of the bike and the hook design distributes the load effectively.
