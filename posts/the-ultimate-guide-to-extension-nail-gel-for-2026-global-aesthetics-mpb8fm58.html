Step-by-Step DIY Extensions with Builder Gel

Using nail polish for gel extensions at home is a straightforward process if you follow the structural "apex" rules. The apex is the thickest part of the nail that prevents snapping.

  1. Preparation: Sanitize and buff the natural nail. Apply a base coat.
  2. Form Placement: Secure the nail form under the free edge.
  3. Application: Use a brush to apply the best UV gel polish brand construction gel, pulling it onto the form to your desired length.
  4. Curing: Cure under a LED/UV lamp for 60-90 seconds.
  5. Refining: Remove the form, wipe with alcohol, and file into your preferred 2026 shape (Almond and Soft Square are trending).

Frequently Asked Questions

What nail polish do you use for gel nails?

For the best results, you should use a dedicated nail polish for gel that is compatible with UV or LED curing systems. While some hybrid polishes exist, professional construction gels like MSHARE provide the structural foundation needed for extensions and long-lasting wear. Always ensure your top coat is also gel-based to seal the design properly.

Can I put gel nails over fungus?

No, you should never apply nail polish for gel or any extension over a fungal infection. Trapping the fungus under a layer of non-breathable gel can worsen the infection and lead to permanent damage. It is essential to treat the infection fully and consult the Healthline medical guides for proper recovery before resuming gel manicures.

Why no nail polish for IV sedation?

While it surprises some, unpolished nails are important because during anesthesia your oxygen is monitored by your nail beds. Polished nail beds could make it harder for the pulse oximeter to read accurate oxygen levels. Medical professionals need to see the natural color of your nail bed to ensure your safety throughout the procedure.

What nail polish is safe for eczema?

If you have eczema on your hands or around your cuticles, you should look for a "10-free" or hypoallergenic professional nail polish for gel. Many modern construction gels are formulated with high-quality resins that lack harsh irritants. However, always perform a patch test first and consult a dermatologist if your skin is currently in an active flare-up.
