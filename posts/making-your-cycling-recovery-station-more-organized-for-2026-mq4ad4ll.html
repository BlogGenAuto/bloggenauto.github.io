Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know which gap width to choose for my garage hooks for tools?

Choosing the right gap depends on what you intend to hang. The 12mm gap is ideal for narrow items like kit bags or gardening shears. The 25mm gap works best for sports gear like rackets or track pumps. For bulky items like foam rollers or power tools, the 45mm gap provides the necessary clearance to prevent snagging.

Can I use these hooks for heavy e-bikes?

Yes, the single and double bike hooks are designed to support heavy loads. While standard hooks might struggle, our heavy duty garage hooks are built with galvanised steel to handle the weight of modern 2026 e-bikes, provided the rail is securely mounted to a solid wall.

Do I need special tools to install these extra hooks?

No special tools are required other than a standard Allen key (hex head), which is used to tighten the CNC machined clamp. The sliding nut allows you to fit the hook anywhere on your existing GearRail™ without any new drilling, making it the perfect DIY upgrade for 2026.

Are the PVC sleeves really necessary for tool storage?

While bare steel is strong, the PVC sleeves are highly recommended for items with delicate finishes. If you are using garage hooks for tools like expensive carbon bikes or lacquered items, the sleeve provides a cushion that prevents vibration damage and surface scratches over time.

What is the benefit of double prong hooks over single hooks?

Double prong hooks effectively double the load capacity and provide much better stability. Items won't swing or tilt as they might on a single hook. This design is what makes them the best garage hooks for tools when you need to store multiple items like several kit bags or heavy sledgehammers on a single mount.
