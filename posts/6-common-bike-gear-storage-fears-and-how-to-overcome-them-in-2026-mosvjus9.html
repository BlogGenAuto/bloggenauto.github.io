What is the best way to hang ladders in a garage?

The best way to store a ladder is with a wall-mounted ladder rack or ceiling storage system. These options keep ladders off the floor, protect them from damage, and save space in your garage or shed. In 2026, using modular ladder hooks garage rails is preferred because it allows you to adjust the spacing between hooks to perfectly fit the rungs of different ladder sizes, ensuring a secure and stable hold.

What are common ladder hook problems?

Most problems come from incorrect hook placement, poor ridge contact, or incompatible hook designs. Fixing these issues usually involves adjusting hook alignment, improving ladder stabilization, and inspecting hook wear before climbing. To avoid these, ensure you are using best ladder hooks garage rated equipment with a strong locking mechanism or a rail-clamping system that prevents the hook from sliding when weight is applied.

How much weight can garage hooks hold?

Hooks with higher weight capacities, like the Heavy Duty Cradle Hook supporting up to 200 lbs, are ideal for heavy tools and equipment. For lighter items, the 5" Single Hook with a 35 lbs capacity may suffice. A variety of weight capacities are available to accommodate different storage needs. GearHooks® products are specifically designed as heavy duty ladder hooks garage solutions, with industrial-grade steel capable of holding 20kg to 25kg per hook depending on the model.

How do I choose the right ladder hooks?

To find the right ladder hook, contact your ladder's manufacturer to see if it has a hook designed to work with its ladders. If it doesn't offer any, review the ladder hook manufacturer's guidelines to see what types of ladders are best suited for its hook. Generally, ladder hooks for van or home use should have a gap wide enough to accommodate the ladder rail but tight enough to prevent swaying, often found in 45mm to 50mm double prong variants.
