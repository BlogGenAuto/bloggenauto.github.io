Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best way to hang ladders in a garage?

Installing heavy-duty wall hooks or ladder racks allows you to securely hang your ladder. To maximize space in a cycling hub, use specialized hooks for ladders in garage that mount to a steel rail. Make sure the ladder accessories such as hooks or racks are rated to handle the weight of your ladder and are anchored into the studs for stability.

What are the alternatives to ladder hooks?

Alternatives include ceiling-mounted hoists or sliding/rolling library ladder systems. The popularity of rolling library ladders inspired the development of a top rolling assembly to replace the hooks. Some people didn't like having to move the hooks off the track and wanted a way to keep the ladder on the track and move it smoothly from place to place. However, for a 2026 cycling hub, simple wall-mounted hooks for ladders in garage remain the most space-efficient and cost-effective choice.

How do I choose the right ladder hooks?

To find the right ladder hook, contact your ladder's manufacturer to see if it has a hook designed to work with its ladders. If it doesn't offer any, review the ladder hook manufacturer's guidelines to see what types of ladders are best suited for its hook. Ensure they are heavy duty hooks for ladders in garage if you are storing a multi-section or fiberglass ladder which can be significantly heavier than aluminum versions.

What are the different types of ladder hooks?

Ladder hooks typically fall into three categories: flat steel, rubberized, and captain styles. Roof ladder hooks come in one of three types: flat steel, rubberized and captain. Flat steel is the most common type. For a shared garage, rubberized hooks are preferred as they prevent the ladder from sliding and protect the finish of both the ladder and the garage wall.
