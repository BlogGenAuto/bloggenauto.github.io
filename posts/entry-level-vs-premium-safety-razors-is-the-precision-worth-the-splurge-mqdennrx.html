Frequently Asked Questions

Are adjustable safety razors better?

Yes, adjustable safety razors are widely considered superior because they offer customization that fixed razors lack. By allowing you to control the blade gap, you can tailor the aggressiveness of the shave to your hair density and skin sensitivity, making them more versatile for different parts of the face and neck.

Why did people stop using safety razors?

In the mid-20th century, the industry shifted toward disposable cartridge razors because of convenience and aggressive marketing. However, this came at the cost of shave quality and environmental health. In 2026, we see a massive return to traditional tools as men prioritize sustainable shaving and superior skin results over quick, low-quality alternatives.

How does an adjustable safety razor work?

An adjustable safety razor works by moving the base plate up or down relative to the top cap. As the numbers on the dial go higher, you'll have more blade reveal or gap. This increase in the gap makes the razor more aggressive, allowing for a closer shave on thick hair. Conversely, lower settings are milder and safer for beginners or sensitive skin.

The "best" razor depends on your needs, but top choices in 2026 include the Merkur Progress for a mild shave, the Rex Ambassador for high-end luxury, and the KWAN YEE GOR Brass Adjustable for a balanced, durable, and ergonomic experience. Beginners often prefer the butterfly open mechanism found in the KWAN YEE GOR model for its ease of use.
