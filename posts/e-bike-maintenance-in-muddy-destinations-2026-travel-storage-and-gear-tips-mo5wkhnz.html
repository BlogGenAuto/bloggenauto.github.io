Cheap hooks often use thin powder coating that chips easily. Once the metal is exposed to moisture from a muddy trail ride, it begins to rust. A zinc-plated garage bike wall rack ensures that your storage solution remains pristine, regardless of how much rain you encounter on your trip.

Why is there a bike rack ban?

The myth of bike rack bans in the United States stems from misunderstandings about legitimate safety regulations rather than any actual prohibition. Bike racks remain completely legal for recreational and commercial use across all 50 states when used in compliance with visibility, lighting, and safety requirements. In 2026, it remains essential to ensure your rack does not obscure your vehicle's license plate or tail lights.

What is the N-1 bike rule?

The N-1 rule is the humorous inverse of the N+1 rule. While the N+1 rule suggests the correct number of bikes to own is always one more than you currently have, the N-1 rule often refers to the limit your partner or living space will allow. However, the technical joke is that the correct number of bikes is n+1, where n is the number currently owned. Is it illegal to drive with a bike rack in the UK?

No, it is not illegal to drive with a bike rack in the UK. However, it is only legal if the rack complies with specific safety laws. The bike rack should not obscure your rear lights (brake lights, hazard lights, etc.) or your number plate. If it does, you must use a lighting board and a secondary number plate to remain compliant with DVSA regulations in 2026.
