Frequently Asked Questions

How much weight can each hook on a hanging hooks garage system hold?

Each individual hook is engineered to support up to 20 kilos, which covers almost all road bikes, mountain bikes, and many modern E-bikes. The total rail capacity is 75 kilos, allowing for a mix of heavy and light bikes to be stored safely. For heavier bikes, we recommend placing them at the ends of the rail to utilize the no-lift rolling feature.

Can I store bikes with mudguards on a vertical bike rack?

Yes, but it depends on the mudguard design. Most standard vertical hooks grip the tyre/rim. If the mudguard stays are flexible or don't cover the very top of the front wheel, it works perfectly. For bikes with full, wrap-around rigid mudguards, you may need to adjust the hook position or look for a specific GearHook designed for mudguard clearance.

Is the 6-bike rack suitable for carbon rims?

Absolutely. Professional storage hooks heavy duty systems include soft rubber sleeves. These sleeves act as a buffer between the steel hook and your rim, preventing any scratches or structural pressure on carbon surfaces. This makes it a safe choice for high-end 2026 road and racing bikes.

How much wall width do I need for 6 bikes?

The "Flying V" design is specifically created to use the least width possible. By staggering the bikes in an inverted V-shape, you can fit 6 bikes in a space significantly narrower than a standard horizontal rack. Generally, a 2-meter wall section is ample for 6 bikes using this staggered method.

Does it work on plasterboard walls?

For the safest installation, we recommend fixing the rail into timber studs (battens) behind the plasterboard. The heavy duty hanging hooks garage rail comes with hex head wall bolts that are perfect for wood. If you must mount on plasterboard, ensuring you hit the vertical studs is critical for supporting the weight of multiple bikes.
