FAQ: Common Questions About Outdoor and Wall Storage

How to secure a bike outside a house?

In all cases, secure your bike using two different gold-standard cycle locks, one through the frame and front wheel; one through the frame and rear wheel. Then protect your bike with a dedicated bicycle cover to keep the worst of the weather away. Using a robust outdoor bike mount wall provides a solid anchor point that makes it much harder for opportunistic thieves to maneuver the bike away from the property.

Can I hang a bike on a wall?

Yes, hanging a bike on a wall is one of the most effective ways to save space. It protects the bike frame from being knocked over and keeps delicate components like derailleurs away from ground-level moisture and dust. In 2026, vertical mounting is the preferred method for professional mechanics and cycling enthusiasts alike.

Can a bike be kept outside?

A bike can be kept outside if it is properly protected from UV rays and moisture. Using a high-quality outdoor bike rack with cover combined with a galvanized steel rail system ensures the storage infrastructure itself won't degrade. However, for long-term maintenance, an indoor or shed-based rail system is always recommended for premium frames.

How to hang bikes on a concrete wall?

To mount to masonry or concrete, you must use fixings that will support several times the weight of the bike. Drill a 5/8” diameter hole at each location 1-3/4” – 2” deep using a masonry drill. Avoid electrical wiring. Slip a lag screw expansion shield into each drilled hole before securing the hex head wall bolts. For the GearRail®, this process typically takes under 10 minutes and provides a rock-solid 100kg capacity.
