Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest way to organize your garage?

The most cost-effective way to organize is to start by decluttering and selling gear you no longer use in 2026. Once you've cleared the space, investing in a high-quality, multi-bike rail system is actually cheaper in the long run than buying individual, low-quality hooks that fail under weight or damage your bikes' rims. A single rail that holds 6 bikes offers a much better value-per-bike than multiple separate mounts.

How to hang stuff in your garage?

To hang items safely, you must identify your wall type—whether it's brick, concrete, or timber studs. For heavy items like 2026 e-bikes, always use industrial-grade wall plugs and heavy-duty hex bolts. Utilizing a horizontal rail system like GearHooks allows you to spread the weight across multiple fixing points, making it much more secure than a single-point hook screwed into drywall.

What items should not be stored in a garage?

You should avoid storing temperature-sensitive items like certain carbon fiber resins (if extreme heat is present) or delicate electronics that aren't moisture-rated. Additionally, hazardous materials like unsealed paint or fuel should be kept in specialized ventilated cabinets. However, your bicycles and technical cycling apparel are perfectly safe in a dry, organized garage utilizing garage organizing hooks that allow for air circulation.

How can I make my garage more organized?

Clean out your garage before organizing to understand your available space. Set up specific zones for maintenance, storage, and cleaning. Hang pegboards or use heavy-duty garage organizing hooks for wall organization to keep the floor clear. Install modular systems like GearHooks for a fully-customized garage that can grow with your 2026 gear collection, and utilize transparent storage containers for smaller components.
