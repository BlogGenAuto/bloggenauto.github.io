Frequently Asked Questions about Strollers with Bassinets

Is it worth getting a bassinet for a stroller?

Yes, getting a stroller with bassinet is highly recommended for the first six months. It provides the necessary lie-flat surface for safe sleeping and healthy spinal development, which standard toddler seats cannot offer. For a second baby, it ensures they have a dedicated, safe space while you manage the older child.

What strollers have a bassinet attachment?

Many premium modular strollers, such as the Venice Child Ventura, come with a bassinet attachment included. These systems are designed to swap between the bassinet and the toddler seat or even use both simultaneously in a double configuration. Other options include 3-in-1 travel systems that allow for car seat, bassinet, and seat transitions.

Are bassinet strollers safe?

Bassinet strollers are very safe when used according to the manufacturer's guidelines and FDA or CPSC standards. They offer a stable, flat surface that prevents airway obstruction in newborns. Always ensure the bassinet is clicked securely into the frame and the baby is never left unattended.

How long can a baby stay in a bassinet stroller?

A baby typically stays in a stroller with bassinet until they can sit up unassisted or reach roughly 22 lbs, which usually happens around 5 to 6 months of age. At this point, you should transition them to the toddler seat with a harness to ensure they remain safely contained as they become more mobile.
