Packing Your Custom Suits: Practical Tips for 2026 Travelers

One of the biggest concerns with custom wedding suits is keeping them pristine during transit. To avoid common 7 Last-Minute Wedding Suit Planning Mistakes to Avoid in 2026, we recommend using the "inside-out" folding method or a high-quality garment bag. If you are flying, many airlines will allow you to hang your suit in the business class closet even if you are seated in economy—it never hurts to ask!

The Art of the Roll

For trousers, rolling them gently alongside other soft items can minimize deep creases. Upon arrival at your destination, hang your suit in the bathroom while you take a hot shower; the steam will naturally relax most minor wrinkles in your wool blend fabric.

What is the difference between a $500 suit and a $5000 suit?

The primary differences are fabric quality, internal construction, and labor. A $500 suit is usually fused (glued) and made from synthetic materials, whereas a $5000 suit is typically full-canvas and hand-sewn from rare wools. Our premium collection bridges this gap by offering high-end wool blends and expert tailoring at a mid-tier price.

What is the 5 suit rule for men?

The 5 suit rule suggests a gentleman should own five foundational suits: Navy, Charcoal, Mid-Grey, a Patterned suit (like pinstripe or plaid), and a Formal suit (Tuxedo). Starting your collection with a navy double-breasted suit is the most efficient way to cover both business formal and evening wear needs.

What does a $1000 suit look like?

A $1000 suit should feature a high-quality drape, clean stitching, and a tailored fit that follows the contours of the body without pulling. It should feel substantial yet breathable and include functional details like real buttonholes on the sleeves and a half-canvas or full-canvas interior for better shape retention.
