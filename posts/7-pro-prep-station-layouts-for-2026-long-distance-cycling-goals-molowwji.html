Frequently Asked Questions

How to hang heavy items on a garage wall?

Mounting things on the garage wall can be as simple as screwing a basic hook into the wall. However, for 2026 standards, a more adaptable and flexible solution is to use a wall-mounted garage wall rack or heavy duty hooks for garage. These comprise a rail or a heavy-duty single anchor that is screwed to the wall with expanding nylon fixings. This ensures the weight of a 50kg e-bike or heavy gear bag is distributed safely without damaging the masonry or timber studs.

How much weight can garage hooks hold?

Hooks with higher weight capacities, like the Heavy Duty Cradle Hook supporting up to 200 lbs, are ideal for heavy tools and equipment. For cyclists, a heavy duty steel hook like the GearHooks® B1plus can hold up to 50kg when the bike's rear wheel stays on the floor, or up to 20kg if the bike is suspended completely. Always ensure your wall type (brick, block, or stud) matches the fixings used to achieve these maximum ratings.

What is the strongest type of hanging hook?

The strongest type of hanging hook is typically one made from cold-formed steel with industrial-grade zinc plating. In most applications, the Secure Gripper Hook and steel cable will exceed your load requirements. For the garage, look for heavy duty hooks for garage that use a CNC machined clamp and a hex head wall bolt, as these provide the most rigid connection to the wall's structure compared to simple screw-in hooks.

What are the strong hooks that don't damage walls?

To prevent wall damage, use hooks that come with high-quality expanding nylon fixings rather than cheap plastic plugs. These spread the load over a larger surface area inside the wall. Additionally, hooks with rubber sleeves protect the items you are hanging (like delicate carbon bike rims), while a wide mounting plate prevents the hook from crushing or marking the plasterboard or brickwork under heavy loads.
