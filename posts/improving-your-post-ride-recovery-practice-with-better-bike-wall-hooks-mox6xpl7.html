Frequently Asked Questions

Can hooks for garage walls hold heavy electric bikes?

Yes, provided you choose heavy-duty models like the GearHooks B1. These are designed to hold bikes up to 20kg off the floor, but more importantly, they allow the back wheel to rest on the ground, supporting the bulk of the e-bike's weight while keeping it organized.

Is vertical bike storage bad for hydraulic brakes in 2026?

Modern hydraulic systems in 2026 are sealed. If your brakes are properly bled, storing them vertically will not cause any issues. In fact, many professional mechanics prefer vertical storage to allow air bubbles to move toward the lever reservoir.

How much wall space do I need for each bike?

Generally, you should allow about 30cm to 40cm of width per bike if they are staggered in height. This prevents handlebars from clashing and ensures you can access each bike without moving the others.

What is the benefit of a no-lift bike hook?

The primary benefit is safety and energy conservation. By keeping the rear wheel on the ground, you avoid the physical strain of lifting, which is crucial for recovery and for users who may have limited upper body strength.

Can I install these hooks on plasterboard walls?

While possible, it is always recommended to fix metal hooks for garage walls into a wooden stud or directly into masonry (brick/block) for maximum security, especially for heavy bikes. GearHooks provides specific industrial-grade fixings for different wall types.
