Shaving Cream vs Gel vs Foam: The Great Debate

Shaving cream is the gold standard for enthusiasts. It provides a rich, thick lather that deeply hydrates the beard. When paired with a brass safety razor, the cream provides enough lift to ensure the blade catches the hair, not the skin. The Modern Shaving Gel

Shaving gels in 2026 have evolved to be highly concentrated and often translucent. This visibility is vital when using an adjustable safety razor to navigate around sensitive areas or style specific beard lines. Gels often contain cooling agents that help soothe skin immediately upon contact.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is shaving with a safety razor better?

The safety razor works with a single, very sharp blade. So you only stress your skin once with each pull and not several times. So even if you conduct a shave with the grain or against and across the grain, your skin will still be less stressed than with a single stroke of your cartridge razor. This leads to fewer ingrown hairs and significantly less irritation over time.

What are the downsides of a safety razor?

Time-consuming: Shaving with a safety razor can take a bit more time compared to using disposable razors or electric shavers. Since safety razors have a single blade, you may need to make multiple passes to achieve a close shave.
