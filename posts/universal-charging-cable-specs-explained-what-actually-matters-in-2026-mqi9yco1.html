Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a universal charger for all phones?

While a single physical plug that fits every phone does not exist globally, a 10-in-1 universal phone charger provides a comprehensive set of adapters that covers roughly 99% of mobile devices. These cables typically include Lightning for iPhone, USB-C for modern Androids, and Micro USB for older gadgets. This makes it the closest solution to a truly "one size fits all" charging system for a modern household.

What is the new universal phone charger called?

The Device Connector (into your Phone) is primarily USB-C: The new universal standard. A small, reversible oval plug. In the context of 2026 regulations, USB-C is the mandated standard for most new electronics. However, multi-head adapters like the 10-in-1 cord are often referred to as "Universal Multi-Port Data Cables" to denote their ability to support legacy ports alongside the new USB-C standard.

Is wireless charging safe for pacemakers?

According to medical research and the FDA, the electromagnetic fields generated by wireless chargers can potentially interfere with pacemakers if they are held too close. Most experts recommend keeping wireless charging pads at least 6 to 12 inches away from implanted medical devices. For those with health concerns, a traditional wired universal phone charger is often a safer and more predictable power source.

What charger for Lenovo Yoga?

Modern Lenovo Yoga laptops typically utilize USB-C Power Delivery (PD) for charging. While a standard 2A universal phone charger can charge the laptop very slowly (or only when powered off), it is primarily designed for mobile phones and peripherals. For laptops, ensure your universal solution specifically supports high-wattage USB-C protocols, though a multi-head cable is excellent for the Yoga's accompanying accessories like wireless mice or pens.
