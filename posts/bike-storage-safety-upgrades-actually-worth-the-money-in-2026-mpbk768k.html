Frequently Asked Questions

How to fix a bike rack to a wall?

To fix a bike rack on wall properly, start by marking the position of each mounting hole (usually 2 per hanger for standard rails or 1 for heavy-duty single hooks). Drill a ¼” diameter hole at each location approximately 1-3/4” – 2” deep, ensuring you avoid electrical wiring. Line up the hanger with the mounting holes and screw in the top lag screw with a flat washer, then secure the bottom to ensure the rack is level and stable for heavy 2026 e-bikes.

Should you hang a bike by the front or back tire?

Both are perfectly fine for the bike's structural integrity, though most people use the front wheel for convenience and better balance. If you are using a vertical bike storage system for space-saving, hanging by the front wheel usually allows for easier access to the gear hooks. Just make sure your hook or hanger is padded with industrial rubber so it doesn't scratch your rim over time.

Is it okay to hang a bike by its tire?

Yes, hanging a bike by its tire is completely safe as the weight of the bike is significantly less than the forces applied during a standard ride. However, in 2026, it is recommended to use hooks with wide contact points and rubber sleeves to distribute the pressure evenly. This prevents any cosmetic damage to carbon rims or the compression of tubeless tire beads.

How to attach a bicycle to a wall?

Attaching a bicycle to a wall requires a high-quality anchor system, such as a CNC machined clamp and an industrial hex bolt. For solid brick or concrete, use expanding nylon fixings to ensure the rack can handle the 20kg-50kg weight limits required for modern cycles. Always ensure the rack is mounted at an appropriate height—either high for space-saving or low for a no-lift setup that protects your back.
